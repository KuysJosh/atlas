EXPLORE THE WONDERS OF LEYTE!

Discover the rich heritage and natural beauty of Leyte's historical sites and landscapes.

Sulyapin at Tangkilikin ang Kahalagahan ng Kultura, Kasaysayan, at Kalikasan ng Leyte!

  • "SAN JUANICO BRIDGE"

    San Juanico Bridge, also known as the Marcos Bridge, is the longest bridge in the Philippines. It spans the San Juanico Strait, connecting the islands of Samar and Leyte.

  • "LEYTE PROVINCIAL CAPITOL"

    The Leyte Provincial Capitol is the seat of government for the province of Leyte. This historic building is an architectural landmark that symbolizes the political and administrative hub of the province.

  • "STO. NIÑO SHRINE"

    The Sto. Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum is a historic and cultural landmark in the Philippines. Originally built as a rest house for former First Lady Imelda Marcos, the shrine features a chapel dedicated to the Sto.

  • "TACLOBAN CITY CONVENTION CENTER"

    Popularly known as the Tacloban Astrodome. It serves as a venue for a wide range of events, including sports competitions, concerts, conventions, and other large gatherings.

  • "STO. NIÑO CHURCH"

    Officially known as the Archdiocesan Shrine of Santo Niño, is a historic Roman Catholic church located in Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines.

  • "TACLOBAN CITY HALL"

    The Tacloban City Hall is the administrative center of Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines. It houses the offices of the city mayor, city council, and various local government departments.
